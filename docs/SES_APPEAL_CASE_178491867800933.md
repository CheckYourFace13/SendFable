Hello,

We are appealing the denial of production access for case 178491867800933 and providing additional information about how Sendfable sends email.

Sendfable (https://sendfable.com) is a permission-based email-marketing platform for small businesses. Customers send newsletters, announcements, and promotional messages only to people who opted in. We also send transactional account messages (for example sign-in links). We do not sell email lists, and we do not send on behalf of customers who use purchased, rented, scraped, harvested, or third-party lists — that use is prohibited.

### Current launch posture (not yet open to the public)

Public signup is currently disabled. Unrestricted campaign sending is currently disabled. After production access is approved, we will run a controlled production-send test with owner-controlled addresses only. General public sending will remain disabled until that test passes.

### Permission and anti-spam controls already implemented

- A public Acceptable Use and Anti-Spam Policy is live at https://sendfable.com/acceptable-use.
- A public contact and abuse-reporting mechanism is live at https://sendfable.com/contact (abuse topic available). Live support mailboxes also exist: support@, abuse@, privacy@, legal@, and security@sendfable.com.
- Purchased, rented, scraped, harvested, and third-party lists are prohibited in our Acceptable Use Policy and Terms.
- A valid physical mailing address is required in campaign footers before sending.
- Unsubscribe links are mandatory. List-Unsubscribe and one-click unsubscribe (RFC 8058) are implemented.
- Hard bounces and spam complaints are automatically suppressed at workspace and platform level.
- Suppressed and unsubscribed contacts cannot be reactivated through CSV imports; import status respects suppression.
- Individual campaigns can be paused or canceled; queued jobs for a paused campaign are drained.
- Workspaces/accounts can be held so sending is blocked.
- New-account sending ramps and plan quotas are enforced in the application.
- Sustained high bounce or complaint rates trigger automatic campaign protection (auto-pause).

### SES identity and event pipeline (already configured)

- Region: us-east-1
- Sending domain identity: send.sendfable.com (verified)
- Easy DKIM: SUCCESS / signing enabled
- Custom MAIL FROM: bounce.send.sendfable.com (SUCCESS)
- Configuration set: sendfable-events
- Event destination publishes to SNS, then to our authenticated HTTPS webhook
- Event types covered: DELIVERY, BOUNCE, COMPLAINT, REJECT, RENDERING_FAILURE, and DELIVERY_DELAY
- Bounce and complaint events update suppression immediately; delivery updates recipient state; processing is idempotent

### Operations

We have monitoring for application health, worker/queue health, stuck or late campaigns, disk usage, TLS expiry, and backup freshness, with owner alerting. An incident procedure covers SES pause, bounce/complaint spikes, spam customers, and related issues.

### Requested initial volume (honest and low)

We request production access with a modest initial send volume appropriate for a careful warm-up:

- First 1–2 weeks after approval: approximately 200–500 messages per day
- Send rate: approximately 1 message per second
- Growth only after clean bounce and complaint metrics

We are not requesting high daily volume at launch. Public signup remains gated while we warm carefully.

### Website and mail type

- Website: https://sendfable.com
- Mail type: MARKETING (permission-based), plus transactional account mail
- Contact language: English
